Theory

What is an MSPD?

Traditional models assume claims arrive independently (Poisson). For cyber risk, past events excite future ones and severity feeds back into frequency. MSPDs capture this.

Key idea: Each event increases the probability of future events through excitation kernels. Larger events produce stronger aftershocks. The framework unifies counting, loss, and intensity processes.

The matrix encodes self- and cross-excitation. The observation kernel determines what is measured (counting, loss, discounted loss…). Marks enter multiplicatively.

Available kernels below: Exponential and Erlang-2 . Marks follow distributions.
